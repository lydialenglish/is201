Education

What I have learned.

Living on a farm has taught me that "free time" is a myth invented by people who don't own livestock. You think you're going to have a peaceful Saturday morning? Nope — the goats just staged a jailbreak, the chickens are plotting something suspicious in the coop, and the tractor decided it’s tired of working harder than you and quit. I've also learned that if you step outside in clean clothes, at least one animal will see it as a direct challenge to your dignity.

I've learned that owning land sounds super majestic until you have to mow it... for six hours... only to realize you missed a patch the size of a small country. And animals? They don’t care if you’re tired, injured, or emotionally fragile. If it's feeding time, you better move like you're in a rodeo. I've pulled muscles I didn’t know existed just trying to wrestle a stubborn sheep into a pen. Forget the gym — farm chores are full-contact CrossFit every single day.

One of the biggest lessons? Nature is not here for your Pinterest aesthetic. I planted a beautiful little vegetable garden, envisioning a peaceful, self-sustaining paradise — and watched it turn into an all-you-can-eat buffet for every rabbit, deer, and possibly UFO in a 10-mile radius. Living on a farm taught me humility, patience, and that fences are merely polite suggestions to most animals. But honestly? Wouldn’t trade it for anything... except maybe a Roomba for outside.
