Skills

This is what I CAN do.

One unexpected skill I've picked up is professional-grade chasing. Forget track practice — try sprinting after a goat that somehow untied itself, jumped a fence, and is now doing parkour off the barn. I can now cut corners like a NASCAR driver, hurdle fallen branches like an Olympian, and make split-second decisions on whether to chase or bribe with snacks. If they gave out medals for "fastest human-goat negotiation," I'd be a world champion.

I've also mastered the ancient art of "angry muttering while fixing stuff." Gates, hoses, fences — if it can break, it will break, usually when you're already tired, hungry, and wearing the wrong shoes. I can now wire, tie, duct-tape, and pray things back together with a skill set that would make MacGyver proud. Bonus points if it holds until after it rains, snows, or the animals discover new and creative ways to destroy it again.

iAnother skill: becoming a walking weather radar. After living on land, I can tell you it’s going to rain because the cows are laying down weird, the chickens are making suspicious noises, and my left knee feels funny. You stop trusting the Weather Channel and start trusting the squirrel population instead. Honestly, if the local news ever needs a backup meteorologist, I’m ready — just don’t expect any fancy maps, just me dramatically sniffing the air and yelling, "Yup, storm’s coming!"
