Experience

This is what I HAVE.

Living on land with animals has taught me more about hard work, patience, and problem-solving than anything else ever could. Every day brings a new challenge — whether it’s fixing a broken fence, helping an animal that's feeling under the weather, or somehow moving 50-pound feed bags across muddy fields without face-planting. There’s so such thing as a predictable day when you live with animals, but that’s part of what makes it so rewarding. You learn quickly how to adapt, stay calm under pressure, and find creative solutions when things (inevitably) go sideways.

I've also gained a huge appreciation for the rhythm of nature and the seasons. Living with animals forces you to pay attention to things most people never think about — the weather forecast becomes your Bible, sunrise and sunset dictate your entire schedule, and you become a walking calendar of planting, harvesting, and hatching seasons. It's a lifestyle that teaches you patience and responsibility in a way that can’t be faked. You’re responsible for real lives every single day, and that shapes how you see the world.

More than anything, living on land has given me a deeper connection to the idea of community — not just with neighbors, but with the animals themselves. Each one has its own personality (some charming, some very stubborn), and you build bonds through daily are and trust. Whether it's laughing at the goat who thinks he’s a dog, or staying up all night to help a new calf stand on shaky legs, you realize pretty quickly that life is bigger, messier, and way more beautiful than you ever imagined.
